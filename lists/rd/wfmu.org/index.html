8/20/18

All Hail the Queen: Aretha Franklin, 1942–2018

Aretha Franklin, one of the towering figures of American music, passed away on August 16th. Of course, her music has meant a lot to soul fans everywhere, and the music fans at WFMU are no exception. In case you missed the various tributes to the Queen of Soul that have aired over the past week, here are links to the tributes that have aired: This Is the Modern World with Trouble, Music to Spazz By with Dave the Spazz, Give the Drummer Some with Doug Schulkind, Sophisticated Boom Boom with Sheila B., Downtown Soulville with Mr. Fine Wine, Todd-O-Phonic Todd, Surface Noise with Joe McGasko, Three Chord Monte with Joe Belock, and Morricone Island with Devon E. Levins. Rest in peace, Aretha.

Wake ‘n Bake Benefit Art Auction on Saturday, October 20th

WFMU’s Wake ‘n Bake Benefit Art Auction comes to Monty Hall Saturday, October 20th and we seek Glistener masterpieces! Artists, fire up your palettes or whatever it is you might use. We’re seeking art that comes out of listening to WFMU, either directly inspired by Wake and Bake, WFMU in general, or not! 5/22/18

We Want Your Records!

Just finished that Marie Kondo book and ready to tidy up your house? Start by donating your unwanted vinyl records and CDs to WFMU. We are now seeking donations of used (but undamaged) record and CD collections to fill holes in our library and to sell at our next Record Fair (April 26-28, 2019). You’ll get a tax break, too! E-mail record and CD wrangler Matt Warwick at mattw@wfmu.org or call him at 201-521-1416, ext. 243.
