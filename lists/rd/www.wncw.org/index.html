Down the Road BRMT | Ola Belle Reed Brought Bluegrass to Baltimore

By WNCW Staff • Aug 20, 2018

Ola Belle Campbell was just a teenager in 1934 when her family moved from Ashe County, NC, to Maryland, but she was already a skilled mountain banjo player and singer. At her first radio gig in 1939, Ola Belle sang the “St. Louis Blues." Her voice overpowered the broadcasting equipment and knocked the station off the air. In 1949 she became Ola Belle Reed when she married country musician Bud Reed. Their influence is still felt in the thriving bluegrass scene around Baltimore.

Jam in the Trees August 24 & 25

By WNCW Staff • Aug 20, 2018

Jam In The Trees will be held on August 24th and 25th this year. The annual music festival returns with a diverse lineup for the third year to Pisgah Brewing Company in Black Mountain, North Carolina. The roster includes legendary artists The Travelin’ McCourys, Jerry Douglas, Shooter Jennings, Elizabeth Cook, Jane Kramer, Gangstagrass, The Stray Birds, Jim Lauderdale, The Steel Wheels, Fireside Collective, The Po’ Ramblin Boys, The Slocan Ramblers, and Andy Buckner.

Friday Feature: Happy Valley Fiddlers Convention

By Paul Foster • Aug 20, 2018

An event that enters its 14th-year for providing music, storytellers, artist competitions, and family events is the Happy Valley Fiddlers Convention in Caldwell County, North Carolina.  Held on an old-family farm between Lenoir and Wilkesboro, the lineup of musicians plays old time and bluegrass music with a focus on the traditions and heritage of that area.  Being held Labor Day weekend.  Chairperson of the event- Tony Deal covers all the details.  Interview originally aired August 17, 2018.
